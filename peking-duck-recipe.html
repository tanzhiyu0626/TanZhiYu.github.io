Ingredients

For Main Dish

  • 1 whole duck (about 2–2.5 kg), cleaned
  • 3 tbsp maltose (or honey)
  • 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tbsp five-spice powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp ginger, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 spring onions, cut into sections

For Serving

  • Mandarin pancakes
  • Hoison sauce
  • Cucumber sticks
  • Spring onions, julienned

Preparation

Step 1/5

Clean the duck thoroughly and pat dry with paper towels. Rub the duck inside and out with salt, five-spice powder, ginger, and garlic. Place spring onions inside the cavity.

Step 2/5

Boil water in a large pot. Pour the hot water evenly over the duck’s skin to tighten it. Pat dry again.

Step 3/5

In a saucepan, mix maltose (or honey), soy sauces, and Shaoxing wine. Brush this glaze all over the duck. Hang or refrigerate the duck uncovered for at least 6 hours (overnight is best) to dry the skin.

Step 4/5

Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F). Place duck on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast for 60–70 minutes, basting occasionally with the glaze, until the skin is golden brown and crispy.

Step 5/5

Remove from oven and let rest for 10 minutes. Carve thin slices of crispy skin and meat. Serve with pancakes, hoisin sauce, cucumber, and spring onions.
